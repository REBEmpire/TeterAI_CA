# TeterAI Construction Administration User Guide

Welcome to the TeterAI Construction Administration app! This guide is designed to help you navigate and use the new AI-powered tools efficiently. We know you’ve been doing this for years, so we’ve built this app not to replace your expertise, but to handle the busywork so you can focus on the real engineering and architecture.

## How the AI Works (And Why You’re Still the Boss)

First things first: **The AI does not send anything to anyone.**

The AI's job is to read incoming documents (RFIs, Submittals, Pay Apps, etc.), cross-reference them with the project specs and history, and *draft* a response or review.

**You have the final say.** Before anything is finalized or sent back to the contractor, a human (you!) must review the AI's draft, make any necessary edits, and click "Approve." We call this a "Human-in-the-Loop" process.

### The Self-Improvement Loop (Grading)
We don't expect the AI to be perfect on day one. That's why we've built in a **Grading** system. When the AI generates a draft, it gets graded. If you heavily edit an AI draft because it missed the mark, the system logs that. You can also manually review AI performance in the "Grading" tab. This feedback goes straight back into the system to train the AI to be smarter and more aligned with your standards for the next time.

## Getting Started: Navigating the App

Whether you are using the Web version or the Desktop version, the main navigation is the same. Look at the top navigation bar to access the different parts of the app:

`[Screenshot: Top Navigation Bar showing Action Dashboard, Upload, Knowledge Graph, etc.]`

### Main Tabs
*   **Action Dashboard:** This is your home base. It shows all pending tasks, documents waiting for your review, and things currently being processed by the AI.
*   **Upload:** Manually upload new documents (PDFs, Word docs) into the system for the AI to process.
*   **Knowledge Graph:** A visual way to search and explore connections between project documents, specs, and history.
*   **Project Intelligence:** Ask questions about your projects and get answers based on the uploaded data.
*   **Pre-Bid Review:** Tools for analyzing documents before the bidding phase to catch issues early.
*   **Closeout Review:** Tools for managing the final stages of a project.
*   **Analysis:** Run deep analyses on specific documents.
*   **Grading:** Where you help train the AI by reviewing its performance.

## Managing Your Inbox (Action Dashboard)

When you log in, start at the **Action Dashboard**. This acts as your intelligent inbox.

`[Screenshot: Action Dashboard showing a list of pending tasks with Urgency badges]`

1.  **Review the Queue:** The dashboard lists tasks (like a new RFI or Submittal). Pay attention to the **Urgency** badges (High, Medium, Low) to prioritize your work.
2.  **Check the Status:** You'll see statuses like "Queued" or "Processing" for things the AI is currently working on.
3.  **Start a Review:** When a task is ready for human review, click on it to open the specific review screen.

## Core Workflows

The AI handles several types of documents. Here is how you interact with them once the AI has finished its initial pass:

### 1. RFIs (Requests for Information)
When you open an RFI from the Dashboard, you'll see the contractor's question alongside the AI's drafted response.
*   Read the AI's draft.
*   Check the references the AI used (it will cite specific sections of the specs).
*   Edit the draft directly if you need to add context or correct the AI.
*   Click **Approve** when it's ready.

`[Screenshot: RFI Review screen showing the split view of the original document and AI draft]`

### 2. Submittals
Similar to RFIs, the AI reviews submittals against the specs.
*   The AI will suggest an action (e.g., "Approved as Noted", "Revise and Resubmit").
*   Review the AI's reasoning and notes.
*   Make your adjustments and hit **Approve**.

`[Screenshot: Submittal Review screen]`

### 3. Pay Apps & Cost Analyses
The AI checks the math, verifies line items against previous pay apps, and flags discrepancies.
*   Review any warnings or "flags" the AI generated.
*   Verify the totals.
*   Approve or reject based on your findings.

### 4. Schedule Reviews
The AI analyzes project schedules to spot delays or logic issues.
*   Review the AI's summary of critical path changes.
*   Use this analysis to quickly formulate your response to the contractor.

## Finalizing: Approving and Exporting

Once you click **Approve** on any task, the app locks in your changes.

Depending on your firm's setup, the approved text can be:
*   Copied to your clipboard to paste into an email or your project management software (like Procore).
*   Exported as a PDF report.

**Remember:** The AI saves you the time of digging through the specs and drafting the initial response. It’s up to you to apply your experience to make sure the final answer is exactly what the project needs.
